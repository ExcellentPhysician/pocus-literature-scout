JAMA · IF ~63 Moderate Relevance
Real-World Outcomes of Transcatheter Tricuspid Valve Replacement: Analysis From the STS/ACC TVT Registry
Makkar RR, Gupta A, O'Neill BP et al. May 12, 2026 PMID: 41973411
This real-world registry study of 1,034 TTVR procedures at 82 US centers confirmed 98.4% TR elimination and significant improvement in NYHA class and KCCQ-OS scores at 30 days, consistent with the TRISCEND II pivotal trial. Echocardiographic assessment was central to confirming TR reduction in all patients. Relevant for EM: severe TR increasingly presents to the ED, and bedside echo assessment of TR severity and hemodynamic impact informs management of these complex patients.
Abstract
Among 1034 attempted procedures at 82 centers (mean age 77.1 years; 69.1% female; 73.2% NYHA class III/IV), a valve was successfully implanted in 1017 patients (98.4%). Mild or less TR was achieved in 98.4% of patients post procedure and in 97.7% at 30 days. At 30 days, all-cause mortality was 3.1%; stroke, 0.2%; bleeding, 7.9%; new CIED, 15.9% in CIED-naive patients; heart failure hospitalization, 3.1%. There were significant improvements in NYHA functional class (class I/II, 82.7%; P<.001) and mean KCCQ-OS score (22.4 points; P<.001) from baseline to 30 days.
Circulation · IF ~35 🔬 High Relevance
Automated Echocardiographic Detection of Congenital Heart Disease Using Artificial Intelligence
Lukyanenko P, Ghelani SJ, Yang Y et al. Mar 28, 2026 PMID: 41902792
EchoFocus-CHD, trained on 3.4 million echo videos from 54,727 studies at Boston Children's Hospital, achieved AUROC 0.94 for detecting critical congenital heart disease with a positive likelihood ratio of 7.50. The model uses a view-agnostic transformer architecture (PanEcho), prioritizing the same views an expert would. This is a landmark demonstration that AI can deliver expert-level CHD detection from echo—directly relevant to POCUS as handheld devices become AI-enabled for automated cardiac screening in resource-limited settings.
Abstract
EchoFocus-CHD showed excellent internal ability to detect critical CHD (AUROC 0.94; PLR 7.50; NLR 0.14) and individual critical lesions (AUROC 0.83-1.00). Performance declined on a referral cohort (AUROC 0.77), coinciding with greater expert disagreement. Retraining on all available US patients improved international critical CHD detection (AUROC 0.87). Explainability analyses demonstrated model prioritization of clinically relevant views across cohorts.

Beattie JA, Kapp CM, Thiboutot J et al. May 21, 2026 PMID: 41956150
A 32-expert international Delphi consensus produced 31 consensus statements on indications, technique, specimen handling, and required expertise for EBUS-guided and peripheral transbronchial cryobiopsy with the 1.1mm cryoprobe. This is the first formal expert guidance for non-ILD applications of EBUS-guided cryobiopsy. Relevant for POCUS practitioners in pulmonary and critical care who use endobronchial ultrasound for procedural guidance and biopsy.
Abstract
An international panel of bronchoscopy experts used a modified Delphi methodology (4 rounds, May-November 2025) covering peripheral transbronchial cryobiopsy, EBUS-guided cryobiopsy, airway/anesthesia/complication management, and specimen handling. Consensus was obtained on 31 statements; no consensus on 17 statements. Consensus defined as ≥80% agreement.
J Ultrasound Med · IF ~3.5 🔬 High Relevance
A Predictive Model for Differential Diagnosis of Cardiogenic and Non-cardiogenic Dyspnea in Patients with Chronic Kidney Disease Based on Cardio and Lung Ultrasound Features
Zhao H, Zhang Z, Long L et al. May 27, 2026 PMID: 42200685
This study combined echocardiographic parameters (E/e', IVC diameter) with lung US B-line patterns and pleural findings to build a diagnostic model distinguishing cardiogenic pulmonary edema from non-cardiogenic dyspnea in CKD patients, achieving AUC 0.97 (training) and 0.89 (validation). A simplified β-weighted scoring system was validated with comparable performance. This directly addresses a core POCUS challenge in the ED and dialysis unit: B-lines are sensitive but non-specific, and integrating cardiac parameters sharply improves specificity.
Abstract
Retrospective study of 215 training and 56 validation CKD stage 3-5 patients. Multivariable logistic regression identified E/e' (OR 1.180), IVC diameter (OR 1.240), bilateral symmetric B-pattern (OR 9.720), bilateral continuous B-pattern (OR 13.777), irregular pleural line ≥1 region (OR 0.078), consolidation ≥1 region (OR 0.039), and pleural effusion ≥1 region (OR 4.732) as independent cardiogenic pulmonary edema predictors. AUC 0.9703 training, 0.8910 validation.
J Ultrasound Med · IF ~3.5 🔬 High Relevance
Point-of-Care Ultrasound for the Diagnosis of Hip Effusion in Children Presenting to the Emergency Department With Atraumatic Limp or Hip Pain: A Scoping Review
Chan N, McCreary DJ, Nedyalkova A, Snelling PJ May 8, 2026 PMID: 42104527
This scoping review of 13 studies confirms that POCUS for pediatric hip effusion achieves sensitivity 80–98% and specificity 91–98% when performed by emergency physicians with variable training. Evidence supports POCUS as accurate for atraumatic limp evaluation and feasible for guiding hip arthrocentesis. Concludes that large prospective multicenter studies are needed to validate POCUS-integrated clinical decision rules for this common pediatric ED presentation.
Abstract
Thirteen studies met inclusion: 3 prospective studies, 9 retrospective cohorts, 1 case report. Studies predominantly evaluated diagnostic accuracy of POCUS performed by emergency medicine physicians vs. radiology ultrasound, and feasibility of POCUS-guided hip arthrocentesis. Diagnostic accuracy high: sensitivity 80%-98%, specificity 91%-98%. Further research required with large prospective multicenter studies.

Eur Heart J Digital Health · IF ~2.5 🔬 High Relevance
Smarter FoCUS: AI-guided focused cardiac ultrasound enables novice detection of left ventricular dysfunction
Kane CJ, Borgeson J, Greason C et al. (Mayo Clinic) May 13, 2026 PMID: 42169733
Prospective study of 496 adults: novice operators with only a 4-hour training used AI-guided FoCUS (Philips Lumify + UltraSight guidance + Mayo Clinic AI interpretation) to detect LVEF <40% with sensitivity 96.2%, specificity 95.4%, and NPV 99.8%—with a median scan time of 4 minutes. A validation cohort of 344 showed sensitivity 100%, specificity 99.4%. This is one of the most directly impactful POCUS papers of the year: AI-guided handheld echo by a non-expert achieves near-flawless LV screening with minimal training.
Abstract
40 subjects (8.3%) exhibited moderate or greater LV dilatation and 28 (5.6%) had LVEF <40%. Adequate views achieved in 95.0% (AI) and 97.4% (expert). Two-step screening—only abnormal AI reads or uninterpretable scans reviewed by experts (15.1%)—achieved sensitivity 96.2%, specificity 95.4%, PPV 54.3%, NPV 99.8%. Prospective validation (n=344): sensitivity 100%, specificity 99.4%.

Deep learning model for genotype prediction from echocardiographic videos in non-ischaemic dilated cardiomyopathy
Kiyohara Y, Fukagawa S, Nomura S et al. May 5, 2026 PMID: 42169734
A deep learning model based on EchoNet-Dynamic predicted genotype positivity in dilated cardiomyopathy from apical 4-chamber echo videos (AUC 0.64), improving to AUC 0.76 when combined with the Madrid clinical scoring system. Demonstrates that echo video contains latent genotype-predictive information extractable by AI. Relevant to POCUS practitioners as future bedside AI tools may guide targeted genetic testing from routine echo acquisitions.
Abstract
258 DCM patients; 117 (45.3%) had genetic variants. Deep learning model AUC 0.64. Madrid genotype score AUC 0.73. Combined model AUC 0.76 (significant improvement from Madrid score alone, p=0.03). Most common genotype was LMNA (30.8%), followed by TTN (18.8%).

Emerging technologies and AI-assisted tools in cardiopulmonary monitoring
Orozco N, Prager R, Arntfield R Apr 30, 2026 PMID: 42019488
This state-of-the-art review from the Western Critical Care group synthesizes AI applications across critical care monitoring, with dedicated focus on how AI enables automated POCUS acquisition guidance and automated cardiac/pulmonary interpretation for novice users. Highlights that AI POCUS models can predict deterioration, hypotension, cardiac arrest, and sepsis hours before clinical manifestation. Notes that fewer than 2% of AI systems have undergone clinical integration—underscoring the gap between research and bedside deployment.
Abstract
Reviews AI applications enhancing waveform interpretation, automating POCUS, enabling predictive monitoring, and extending advanced assessment into low-resource settings. AI models identify deterioration earlier than conventional tools and predict hypotension, cardiac arrest, and sepsis hours in advance. In POCUS, AI enables real-time acquisition guidance and automated cardiac/pulmonary interpretation, allowing novice users to obtain expert-quality studies. Fewer than 2% of AI systems remain clinically integrated.

Diagnostic performance of artificial intelligence in fracture detection with ultrasound: A systematic review and meta-analysis
Xie M, Kim JJ, Im JH et al. (University of Alberta) May 20, 2026 PMID: 42163701
Meta-analysis of 8 studies evaluating deep learning for bone fracture detection on ultrasound images (predominantly pediatric upper extremity fractures in the ED). Pooled sensitivity 0.77 (95% CI 0.69–0.83), specificity 0.90 (95% CI 0.86–0.94); patient-level analyses yielded sensitivity 0.89 and specificity 0.94 with sROC AUC 0.91. AI-assisted POCUS fracture detection is highly specific—sufficient to reliably identify fractures in high-pretest-probability pediatric ED cases, potentially reducing X-ray exposure.
Abstract
580 papers identified; 8 studies included. 21 reported model evaluations from 5 studies with extractable data. Pooled sensitivity 0.77 (95% CI 0.69-0.83), specificity 0.90 (95% CI 0.86-0.94), overall sROC AUC 0.91. Patient-level evaluations: sensitivity 0.89 (0.81-0.94), specificity 0.94 (0.81-0.98). Risk of bias assessed via QUADAS-2. Predominantly pediatric cohorts presenting with suspected upper extremity fractures.

Modified lung ultrasound score and related findings in children with Mycoplasma pneumoniae pneumonia
Xie W, Lin W, Zhuang Q, Lyu G May 21, 2026 PMID: 42163724
In 186 children with pneumonia, mycoplasma etiology was associated with a significantly higher modified lung US score (median 15 vs 8–9 for viral/bacterial), more extensive solid lung lesions, older age, and fever without bronchospasm. These sonographic features can be recognized in real-time at the bedside. Practical implication: a high MLUS with extensive consolidation in a school-age febrile child without wheezing should raise clinical suspicion for Mycoplasma, potentially guiding empiric macrolide therapy.
Abstract
186 children enrolled (Jan-Dec 2023): mycoplasma (n=74), viral (n=63), bacterial (n=49) pneumonia. Median MLUS: mycoplasma 15 (IQR 10-22) vs bacterial 9 (5-16) vs viral 8 (5-15), P<0.05. Mycoplasma group: more febrile symptoms, extensive solid lung lesions, fewer asthmatic symptoms, older median age 6 years vs 3 years for others.
The Ultrasound Journal · IF ~2 🔬 High Relevance
Point-of-care ultrasound training using near-peer training and remote supervision: A pilot randomized controlled trial
Hsu NC, Yang CW, Lin YF et al. (National Taiwan University Hospital) May 20, 2026 PMID: 42159600
In a pilot RCT, 69 medical students randomized to near-peer teaching + remote faculty supervision vs. traditional faculty-led POCUS training showed equivalent overall OSCE scores (74.9 vs 76.8, p=0.614) and equivalent domain scores across indication, acquisition, interpretation, and medical decision-making. The near-peer group reported higher perceived usefulness and were more likely to endorse routine post-training OSCE assessment. Near-peer + remote POCUS training is non-inferior and highly scalable—directly addressing the global POCUS workforce gap.
Abstract
69 senior medical students: NPT+RS (n=34) vs faculty-led (n=35). Total OSCE scores (74.9 vs 76.8, p=0.614). Domain scores did not differ. Single item: lower interpretation performance for posterolateral alveolar/pleural syndrome in NPT+RS group (4.6 vs 6.5, p=0.013). NPT+RS group: higher perceived usefulness (all p<0.05), more often perceived OSCE as revealing weaknesses (p=0.002).
The Ultrasound Journal · IF ~2 🔬 High Relevance
Progress in lung ultrasound education: An updated systematic review
Borggaard PG, Kildegaard C, Laursen CB et al. May 19, 2026 PMID: 42153633
PRISMA systematic review of 68 studies (2017–2025) on thoracic US training methods across specialties. Most interventions improved test scores; class-based + hands-on training was most common. Critical finding: assessment validity was rarely addressed, and translation to safe clinical use is poorly studied across all modalities. Provides a comprehensive benchmark for where TUS education programs succeed and where they fall short.
Abstract
12,460 studies screened; 68 included. Physicians primary audience in 65%; 29% other healthcare professionals. Class-based teaching most common (66%), sometimes combined with web-based (16%). 84% involved practical training on humans (63%). 10 RCTs, 4 non-randomized comparison studies identified. Most tools led to significant improvement in test scores; assessment validity rarely addressed.
The Ultrasound Journal · IF ~2 🔬 High Relevance
Diagnostic accuracy of ultrasound in assessing medial collateral ligament and medial meniscus injuries: A systematic review and meta-analysis
Potter M, Smith A (University of Essex) May 18, 2026 PMID: 42148467
Meta-analysis of 14 studies (MCL: n=713, MM: n=969) showed ultrasound has high specificity for MCL injuries (sensitivity 0.83, specificity 0.96, AUC 0.90) and moderate sensitivity for medial meniscus tears (sensitivity 0.85, specificity 0.89, AUC 0.88). These data support POCUS as a practical, low-cost initial screening tool in the ED for medial knee injury evaluation, particularly for MCL where specificity is sufficient to guide immediate management without awaiting MRI.
Abstract
MCL: 9 studies (713 patients), pooled sensitivity 0.83 (0.76-0.88), specificity 0.96 (0.91-0.98), DOR 117.8, AUC 0.90, I²=0%. Medial Meniscus: 12 studies (969 patients), sensitivity 0.85 (0.82-0.88), specificity 0.89 (0.81-0.94), DOR 45, AUC 0.88, I²=70.6% for specificity. Overall risk of bias low; moderate certainty for MCL, low for MM.
The Ultrasound Journal · IF ~2 🔬 High Relevance
Prediction of post-extubation stridor using ultrasound measurement of subglottic airway diameter
Jongthitinon N, Saelim K, Kritsanepaiboon S et al. May 7, 2026 PMID: 42095341
In 176 PICU children (PES rate 18.75%), intracricoid peritubular free space (IPFS) <2.08mm by subglottic ultrasound showed 80% sensitivity and 65% specificity for predicting post-extubation stridor—outperforming both subglottic ratio and leak percentage testing. This practical POCUS application in pediatric critical care provides a non-invasive pre-extubation assessment tool. IPFS measurement is straightforward and could be readily incorporated into extubation readiness bundles.
Abstract
Prospective study in university-affiliated PICU, children aged 1 month-15 years. PES in 33/176 (18.75%) patients. Subglottic ratio <1.30: sensitivity 70%, specificity 65%, accuracy 66%. IPFS <2.08mm: sensitivity 80%, specificity 65%, accuracy 68%. Leak percentage <36.77% at 25 cmH₂O: sensitivity 80%, specificity 47%, accuracy 53%. AUC values: SR 0.664, IPFS 0.691, leak percentage 0.648.
IEEE Trans Med Imaging · IF ~2 Moderate Relevance
Echocardiography Video Segmentation via Mamba-based Spatiotemporal Synergistic Network and Adaptive-dynamic Learning
Sun Y, Chen G, Guo J, Wu H May 27, 2026 PMID: 42202178
MSSNet applies the Mamba sequence modeling architecture to semi-supervised echocardiographic video segmentation, outperforming current SOTA methods on CAMUS and EchoNet-Dynamic benchmarks while improving inference speed for real-time clinical deployment. The spatiotemporal synergistic guidance module uses historical frame context to handle speckle noise and dynamic ventricular changes. This technical advance will underpin the next generation of automated POCUS measurement tools at the bedside.
Abstract
Proposes MSSNet with spatiotemporal synergistic guidance (SSG) module, region-wise adaptive cross-mix (RAC) and dynamic offset correction (DOC) for semi-supervised echo segmentation. Experiments on CAMUS and EchoNet-Dynamic demonstrate superior segmentation accuracy and notable improvements in inference speed vs. SOTA. Code available at https://github.com/SSS666-klk/MSSNet.
